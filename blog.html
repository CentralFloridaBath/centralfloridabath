5 Tips to Prepare for a Shower/Bath Remodel

Posted: Feb 2025 • Read time: 3 min

Remodeling your bathroom can be exciting, but without proper preparation, it may quickly feel overwhelming. A little planning upfront ensures the process is smoother and less stressful.

1) Set a realistic budget. Include a 10–15% buffer for surprises such as hidden water damage, plumbing updates, or structural repairs. This keeps you from being blindsided mid-project. FYI Most minor repairs are included in our service.

2) Decide your must-haves vs. nice-to-haves. Prioritize upgrades that directly improve function, safety, and resale value. Then, list “dream” features that you’ll add if budget allows.

3) Choose materials early. Advanced PVC Tile, Virgin acrylic wall systems, and fixtures can have long lead times. By deciding on finishes before demo begins, you avoid costly delays and rushed decisions.

4) Clear the space. Remove towels, toiletries, rugs, and wall décor. Give installers a blank canvas to work with, and protect floors or hallways leading to the bathroom.

5) Plan for dust and access. Remodeling always involves dust. Cover nearby furniture, and if you have kids or pets, set up safe boundaries away from the work area. We use a drop cloth from the entrance all the way to the bathroom to help ensure the job stays are clean as possible.

With these simple steps, you’ll be better prepared for the remodel process — and enjoy a faster, cleaner installation. For a free consultation, our team is ready to help you plan with confidence.

Walk-In Shower vs. Tub: What’s Right for You?

Posted: May 2025 • Read time: 4 min

One of the most common questions homeowners face during a remodel is whether to upgrade the tub or convert it into a modern, walk-in shower. Both options have unique benefits depending on your lifestyle and long-term goals.

Walk-in showers are popular for their sleek, modern look and ease of maintenance. They’re especially beneficial for homeowners who want better accessibility, since they reduce the risk of slips and falls with low thresholds.

Tubs remain a great choice for families with small children or those who enjoy a relaxing soak at the end of the day. However, tubs often take more space and can be harder to clean over time.

Many homeowners today choose a middle ground: converting one tub into a walk-in shower while keeping another in the home for resale appeal. This way, you get the accessibility of a shower and the flexibility of a tub when needed.
